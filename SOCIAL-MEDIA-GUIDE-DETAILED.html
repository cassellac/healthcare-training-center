Social Media Templates & Guide

Step-by-step instructions for creating and sharing Healthcare Training Center posts

Hello! 👋 This guide is designed to help anyone create beautiful, on-brand social media posts even if you've never done this before. Each template below includes:

LinkedIn Professional Announcement

Best for: New course launches, professional updates, company news
🚀 Room 2 is Live
HIPAA & Privacy Training

📖 What is this?

LinkedIn is a professional networking site where healthcare workers, hospital administrators, and compliance officers hang out. This template is for announcing big updates — like when a new training room launches or a big milestone is reached.

🎯 When to use it:

Room Launch
Post when Room 1, 2, 3, 4, or 5 goes live for the first time
Milestone Achievement
"We've trained 500 healthcare professionals!" or "Our platform is now live in 10 states"
Testimonial
Share a success story: "This training helped our entire team understand WHY compliance matters"
Thought Leadership
Share an insight: "Why HIPAA training shouldn't be boring" or "The hidden cost of poor compliance"

✏️ How to create this (Step-by-Step):

1
Go to LinkedIn and Log In
Visit linkedin.com, log into your account. If you don't have one, create a free account with your email.
2
Click "Start a Post"
At the top of your LinkedIn feed, you'll see a text box that says "Start a post". Click on it.
3
Write your post text
Click in the text box and type your message. Example:
🚀 NEW COURSE ALERT: Room 2 (HIPAA & Privacy) is now live! Protect patient information. Prevent breaches. Understand the rules. This 45-minute course covers: ✓ HIPAA Privacy Rule compliance ✓ How to identify and report a potential breach ✓ Real-world scenarios and outcomes Your patients deserve your best. We're here to help you deliver it. Step inside → [link] #HealthcareTraining #HIPAA #ComplianceFirst
4
Add an image (optional but recommended)
Click the "image" icon in the post box. Upload a screenshot from the BRAND-GUIDE.html showing the room colors and title, or take a screenshot of Room 2 on your website.
5
Click "Post"
Once you're happy with your text and image, click the blue "Post" button. Your post is now live!
💡 Pro Tip: LinkedIn has the best engagement with healthcare professionals between 8–10am and 5–6pm on weekdays. Post during these times if you want more people to see it.

🛠️ Tools to make it easier:

LinkedIn Native

Cost: Free

The easiest way — just type directly in LinkedIn. No extra tools needed.

Buffer

Cost: Free or paid

Schedule posts to go out at the perfect time. buffer.com

Canva

Cost: Free or paid

Create beautiful graphics for your post. canva.com

Instagram Carousel Post (Multi-Slide)

Best for: Breaking down concepts, room features, step-by-step tips
Swipe through all 5 rooms →

📖 What is this?

An Instagram carousel is a post with multiple slides (like a mini-slideshow). People can swipe left to see the next slide. It's perfect for showing all five training rooms, or breaking down a topic into steps.

🎯 When to use it:

Show All 5 Rooms
One slide per room: Room 1 (Customer Service), Room 2 (HIPAA), etc. Each slide shows the room color, icon, and a brief description.
Educational Content
5 tips for better healthcare communication, 4 steps to HIPAA compliance, etc.
Storytelling
Share the founder's story across multiple slides: "Here's why I built this..." Slide 2: "The problem was..." Slide 3: "Now we're..."

✏️ How to create this:

1
Use Canva (easiest option)
Go to canva.com, sign up for free (or log in). Search for "Instagram Post" template. You'll see tons of designs you can customize.
2
Create Slide 1: Title
Pick a template design you like. Change the colors to navy (#1E3A6E) and teal (#2B8FA9). Add text like "Meet the 5 Training Rooms ↓" or "Slide through all 5 rooms →"
3
Duplicate and customize for each room
In Canva, click "Duplicate" to copy the slide. Change the title to "Room 1: Customer Service" and the color to sky blue (#3B7DD8). Repeat for all 5 rooms with their respective colors.
4
Download all slides as images
Click "Download" and choose "PNG". Canva will download each slide as a separate image. You'll have 5 images: Slide1.png, Slide2.png, etc.
5
Upload to Instagram
Open Instagram on your phone or computer. Click "Create" → "Post". Upload all 5 images in order. Instagram will automatically turn them into a carousel. Add your caption and post!
💡 Pro Tip: Instagram carousels get 3x more engagement than single-image posts! People love being able to swipe through. Use this format whenever you can.
⚠️ Important: Instagram requires images to be 1080×1350px (taller than they are wide). Canva's "Instagram Post" template is already this size, so you're good!

🛠️ Tools you'll need:

Canva

Cost: Free (with optional paid upgrades)

Design carousel slides without any experience. canva.com

Instagram

Cost: Free

Upload and share your carousel. instagram.com

Instagram Story (Quick Daily Updates)

Best for: Quick announcements, daily tips, behind-the-scenes
Today's Tip 💡

📖 What is this?

Instagram Stories are quick posts that disappear after 24 hours. They're perfect for casual, frequent updates. Stories show up at the top of people's feeds and feel more immediate and personal than regular posts.

🎯 When to use it:

Daily Tips
Share one quick HIPAA tip, compliance fact, or healthcare insight every day
Announcements
"Room 3 launches tomorrow!" or "Join us for a live Q&A Thursday at 2pm"
Behind-the-Scenes
Show the team working on courses, quick interviews with your mom, sneak peeks of new content
Polls & Engagement
Ask your audience questions: "Which topic should we cover next?" Use Instagram's built-in poll sticker

✏️ How to create this:

1
Open Instagram
On your phone, open Instagram and look at the top of your feed. You'll see circles with profile pictures. Click the "+" in the top-left to start a new story.
2
Pick a background color
Click the "Color" tool at the bottom. Pick navy (#1E3A6E) or one of the room colors (#3B7DD8, #2BA89A, #2EAA6E, #7B5EA7, #D4A017). Hold your finger on the color to select a custom color.
3
Add text
Click the "Aa" text tool. Type your message. Example: "💡 HIPAA Tip: Patient privacy isn't just compliance — it's a right."
4
Customize the text
Triple-tap the text to select all of it. Change the font (try "Modern" or "Strong"). Change the text color to white or another room color. Make it big and bold so it's easy to read.
5
Click "Send To" and share
Click the arrow at the bottom. Select "Your Story". Your story is now live for 24 hours! After 24 hours, it disappears automatically.
💡 Pro Tip: Post stories 1–2 times per day. They don't fill up your followers' feeds like regular posts, and people expect stories to be frequent and casual. Don't stress about making them perfect!

🛠️ All you need:

Just Instagram on your phone. No extra tools needed — the built-in color and text tools are perfect for stories.

Email Signature Template

Best for: Every email your mom sends
Your Mom's Name
Founder, Healthcare Training Center
hello@healthcaretrainingcenter.com
www.healthcaretrainingcenter.com

📖 What is this?

Every email your mom sends should include a professional footer with her name, title, and contact info. This is called an email signature. It's automatically added to the bottom of every email she writes.

🎯 Why use it:

  • Looks professional in every email
  • Always includes the company website (great for promotion)
  • People know how to reach her
  • Reinforces brand colors and style

✏️ How to set up an email signature:

1
Open your email (Gmail, Outlook, etc.)
This example uses Gmail. Log in at gmail.com
2
Click the gear icon ⚙️ and go to Settings
In the top-right corner of Gmail, click the gear icon. Select "See all settings".
3
Go to the "Signature" section
Scroll down until you see "Signature". Click the "Create new" button.
4
Copy this text and customize it
Your Mom's Name Founder, Healthcare Training Center hello@healthcaretrainingcenter.com www.healthcaretrainingcenter.com Step Inside. Step Up.
Type this into the signature box, replacing "Your Mom's Name" with her actual name and the email/website with the correct ones.
5
Click "Save Changes"
Scroll down and click "Save Changes". Now every email your mom writes will automatically include this signature!
💡 Pro Tip: Email signatures don't have color in Gmail's simple text editor, but they look professional and include all the important contact info. That's what matters!

Infographic Post (Shareable Graphic)

Best for: Statistics, facts, educational content
5 HIPAA Facts
You Need to Know

📖 What is this?

An infographic is a visual way to share information. Instead of writing text, you show facts, numbers, or tips with colors, icons, and simple text. These posts get shared a lot because they're easy to understand and look professional.

🎯 When to use it:

5 Facts About Each Room
"5 HIPAA facts every healthcare worker should know"
Statistics
"73% of healthcare teams say our training improved their confidence"
Checklists
"The 7-point compliance checklist" or "5 red flags for PHI breaches"
Timeline
Show the journey from Lobby → Room 1 → Room 2, etc.

✏️ How to create this:

1
Go to Canva and search for "Infographic"
Visit canva.com, log in (free account), and search for "infographic". Pick a template you like.
2
Change the colors to match the brand
Find the color elements (boxes, backgrounds, etc.). Click on them and change the colors to navy (#1E3A6E), teal (#2B8FA9), or a room color.
3
Update the text
Replace the placeholder text with your facts or tips. Example:
HIPAA FACT #1: The HIPAA Privacy Rule governs how patient information is protected.
4
Change the icons
Click on any icons in the template. Canva has a search feature — search for Phosphor icons (like "shield", "chart", "lock") to replace them with brand-appropriate icons.
5
Download and share
Click "Download" and choose "PNG". Then share it on LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook, or Twitter. People love sharing infographics!
💡 Pro Tip: Infographics are highly shareable. People will repost them, which helps spread the word about Healthcare Training Center. Make them visually interesting and easy to understand!

🛠️ Tools you'll need:

Canva

Cost: Free (with optional paid upgrades)

Tons of infographic templates. canva.com

Troubleshooting & Common Questions

❓ "I don't know what to post about. What should I say?"

Here are some easy post ideas:

  • Room launches: "Room X is live! Here's what you'll learn..."
  • Daily tips: Share one quick fact or tip related to the rooms
  • Questions for followers: "What's the biggest compliance challenge in your organization?"
  • Milestones: "We've trained 100 healthcare professionals!" or "Our platform is now in 5 states!"
  • Founder story: "Here's why I built this..." Tell your mom's story in bits and pieces
  • Testimonials: "This training helped our team understand WHY compliance matters" — quote from a user

❓ "I don't have an image for my post. What do I do?"

Option 1: Use Canva to create one (easiest).
Option 2: Take a screenshot of your website or the BRAND-GUIDE.html and use that.
Option 3: Just post text — LinkedIn is fine with text-only posts!

❓ "How do I know if my post is working?"

Check your analytics:

  • LinkedIn: After posting, you'll see "impressions" (people who saw it) and "engagement" (likes, comments, shares)
  • Instagram: Tap the post → tap "View Insights". You'll see how many people saw it and who engaged
  • General rule: If a post gets more than 5 likes or 1 comment, it's working! Share more like it.

❓ "This is too much. What's the bare minimum I should do?"

Start here and build from there:

  • Week 1: Set up an email signature (takes 5 minutes). You're done!
  • Week 2: Post 1 LinkedIn announcement about a room launch. That's it!
  • Week 3: Add 2 Instagram stories with quick tips. Easy!
  • Once you're comfortable, add more. No pressure!

❓ "Can I schedule posts in advance?"

Yes! Use these free/cheap tools:

  • LinkedIn: You can schedule posts directly in LinkedIn (premium feature, but worth it)
  • Instagram: Use Buffer (buffer.com) or Later (later.com) to schedule posts in advance
  • Multiple platforms: Use Buffer to schedule to LinkedIn, Instagram, Twitter all at once

You've Got This! 🎉

Social media doesn't have to be scary. Start simple, post regularly, and watch your audience grow.
